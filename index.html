Music Where words fail music speaks

by Harshita Mittal

Embrace the golden era of 60-90s hindi music. The placid spirit of this classic music collection is immortal.

We remember the power of love in Pyar kiya to darna kya by Lata Mangeshkar from the Bollywood epic 'Mughal-e-zam'(1960) , revitalizing patriotism in Ay mere pyare watan by Manna De from Kabuliwala (1961), celebration of friendship in Yeh Dosti by Kishore Kumar from 'Sholay' (1975) and the concept of modern love in Tujhe dekha to yeh jana by Lata Mangeshkar from the romantic blockbuster hit 'Dilwale Dulhania Le Jayenge'(1995).

Listening to this music, is miraculous with the background music creating the exact picture of surroundings and the lyrics revealing every feeling of your senses. The 60's saw the beginning of music blended with orchestra, followed by rock and roll fusion in 70's and early 80's whereas 90's pioneered with the pop music.

So, wish to be enthralled by some harmonious magic, get on board for this reviving journey of sixties to nineties music.

Lata Mangeshkar

Indian film playback singer, and occasional music-composer. She is one of the best-known and most respected playback singers in India.

Mangeshkar's career started in 1942 and has spanned over seven decades.

Mohd. Rafi

Indian playback singer and one of the most popular singers of the Hindi film industry. Rafi was notable for his versatility, his songs ranged from classical numbers to patriotic songs, sad lamentations to highly romantic numbers, qawwalis to ghazals and bhajans.

Between 1950 and 1970, Rafi was the most sought after singer in the Hindi film industry.

Kishore Kumar

Indian film playback singer, actor, lyricist, composer, producer, director, and screenwriter.He is considered one of the most successful playback singers of the Hindi film industry.

Apart from Hindi, he sang in many Indian languages including Bengali, Marathi, Assamese, Gujarati, Kannada, Bhojpuri, Malayalam, Odia, and Urdu.
